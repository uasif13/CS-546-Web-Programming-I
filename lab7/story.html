Admirable Asif

Asif Uddin had always loved grand Exeter with its curvy, chubby cliffs. It was a place where he felt stable.

He was an admirable, snotty, brandy drinker with curvaceous spots and slimy arms. His friends saw him as an abundant, alert author. Once, he had even brought a warty old lady back from the brink of death. That's the sort of man he was.

Asif walked over to the window and reflected on his creepy surroundings. The wind blew like skipping pigeons.

Then he saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Gary Smart. Gary was a considerate friend with skinny spots and short arms.

Asif gulped. He was not prepared for Gary.

As Asif stepped outside and Gary came closer, he could see the puny glint in his eye.

Gary glared with all the wrath. He said, in hushed tones, "I hate you and I want Revenge."

Asif looked back, even more concerned and still fingering the tattered map. "Gary, I am your father," he replied.

They looked at each other with worried feelings, like two clumsy, chubby cats cooking at a very hungry snow storm, which had R & B music playing in the background and two giving uncles bopping to the beat.

Asif regarded Gary's skinny spots and short arms. "I feel the same way!" revealed Asif with a delighted grin.

Gary looked relaxed, his emotions blushing like a repulsive record.

Then Gary came inside for a nice glass of brandy.
